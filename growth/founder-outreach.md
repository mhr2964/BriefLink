# BriefLink founder outreach

## Purpose
Founder-led outreach for early agency and consultancy prospects.

## Core positioning
BriefLink helps agencies and consultancies bring more clarity to client work by turning scattered inputs into a clearer, shareable brief.

Use it as:
- a clarity-first tool for messy client communication
- a way to reduce confusion between conversation and execution
- a lightweight approach for custom client work

Do not position it as:
- a CRM
- a generic AI assistant
- a project management platform
- a fully featured operations suite

## Recommended audience
- Founder
- Managing Partner
- Agency Owner
- Principal
- Operations Lead
- Client Services Lead
- Delivery Lead

## Personalization tokens
- `{first_name}`
- `{firm_name}`
- `{specialization}`
- `{shared_connection}`
- `{sender_name}`

## Recommended timing
| Step | Day | Channel | Goal |
|---|---:|---|---|
| 1 | 1 | Email | Establish relevance |
| 2 | 4 | Email | Clarify problem and value |
| 3 | 8 | DM | Light reminder |
| 4 | 12 | Email | Close loop |

## Sequence

### Email 1
**Subject options**
- Quick thought for `{firm_name}`
- A clearer way to handle client input
- Reaching out about client clarity at `{firm_name}`

Hi `{first_name}`,

I’m reaching out because teams like `{firm_name}` often deal with client input spread across calls, notes, messages, and docs.

BriefLink is built for agencies and consultancies that want a clearer way to turn that input into something the team can share and act on.

Thought this might be relevant given your work in `{specialization}`. If improving clarity around client work is a current priority, I’m happy to send a short overview.

— `{sender_name}`

### Email 2
**Subject options**
- Is this a problem for your team?
- When client input gets messy
- Quick follow-up

Hi `{first_name}`,

Following up because the issue we keep hearing is simple:

client input arrives in fragments, teams interpret it differently, and clarity drops between conversation and delivery.

That’s the problem BriefLink is meant to help with.

If helpful, I can send a quick example of where it tends to fit for agency and consultancy teams.

— `{sender_name}`

### DM follow-up
Hey `{first_name}` — quick follow-up in case email got buried. We’re building BriefLink for agencies and consultancies that want a clearer way to turn scattered client input into something structured and shareable. Happy to send the short version if useful.

### Email 3
**Subject options**
- Worth a quick look?
- Should I close the loop?
- Final note

Hi `{first_name}`,

Last note from me.

I reached out because BriefLink seems most relevant for firms where client work can get messy between conversation and execution. If that’s not a current priority, no problem.

If it is relevant, just reply:
- **show me** and I’ll send the short overview
- **later** and I’ll circle back another time

— `{sender_name}`

## Follow-up logic
- Positive reply: send overview or next-step link and stop sequence
- “Later”: snooze for 30–45 days
- Not relevant: mark closed-lost
- No reply after final step: recycle to low-priority nurture
- Skip DM if no active social profile is available
