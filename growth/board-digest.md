# BriefLink Growth GTM Packet

## 1) Top 3 acquisition experiments for the next 2 weeks

### Experiment 1 — Cold outbound teardown offer to agencies/consultancies
- **Channel:** founder/GTM lead email + LinkedIn follow-up
- **Target audience:** 5–50 person agencies and consultancies already sending proposals, decks, case studies, onboarding docs, and status updates
- **Hypothesis:** buyers who already feel document/link sprawl pain will reply to a concrete “we reviewed your client-facing materials” angle faster than to generic product outreach
- **Creative angle:** “We found 3 places your client links create friction — want a 5-minute teardown?”
- **Offer:** free teardown of one live client-facing resource flow (proposal + deck + docs + next-step links)
- **Why this fits profitable/reachable buyers:** agencies and consultancies have recurring client communication needs, fast feedback loops, and clearer ROI than broad SMB
- **Success metric (2 weeks):** 10%+ positive reply rate on targeted list; 8 teardown calls booked; at least 3 buyers confirm current workflow pain in their own words
- **Execution notes:** start with 50 highly relevant targets from visible agency sites and LinkedIn; personalize first line using visible client resource patterns

### Experiment 2 — LinkedIn “client handoff chaos” founder content + direct CTA
- **Channel:** founder LinkedIn posts + comment CTAs + DM follow-up
- **Target audience:** agency owners, account leads, consultants, and ops leads
- **Hypothesis:** practical content about client-facing link chaos will create warmer demand than feature-led posting, especially when paired with a teardown CTA
- **Creative angle:** short posts showing “before/after” examples of scattered proposal/deck/doc experiences versus a single briefing link
- **Content themes:**
  - “Why clients stop replying after the proposal stage”
  - “The hidden cost of sending 6 links in one email”
  - “How agencies make simple projects feel messy without noticing”
- **CTA:** “Comment ‘teardown’ and I’ll send a quick audit template” or “DM me ‘brief’ for an example”
- **Success metric (2 weeks):** 5+ inbound conversations; 2+ qualified agency calls; at least one post with meaningful engagement from agency operators or founders
- **Execution notes:** publish 4–6 posts over 2 weeks; reply to every agency/commenter manually; DM only engaged prospects

### Experiment 3 — Agency operator communities with audit template lead magnet
- **Channel:** niche Slack groups, agency communities, consultancy/operator groups, founder circles, and direct partner introductions
- **Target audience:** agency operators and client service leaders who own process consistency
- **Hypothesis:** a lightweight “client handoff audit” asset earns more engagement in communities than direct product promotion
- **Creative angle:** “We made a 1-page client handoff audit to spot where proposals, docs, and next steps break trust”
- **Asset:** downloadable/checklist-style audit for reviewing a client-facing workflow
- **CTA:** “If you want, I’ll score one real client flow with you live”
- **Success metric (2 weeks):** 20 audit downloads/shares, 5 follow-up conversations, 2 live workflow reviews
- **Execution notes:** avoid broad startup communities; prioritize agency ops and consultancy groups where operational pain is already discussed

## 2) ICP and wedge

### Primary ICP
- 5–50 person agencies and consultancies
- Client-service-heavy businesses with repeatable but messy handoffs
- Teams sending proposals, decks, docs, forms, calendars, case studies, and updates across multiple tools
- Buyers likely include founder, operations lead, account lead, or delivery lead

### Pain wedge
- They do not have a “create more content” problem
- They have a **client-facing clarity and trust** problem caused by scattered links and inconsistent handoffs
- Every proposal, kickoff, or update asks clients to piece together context from too many assets
- This makes the agency feel less organized than the actual quality of its work

### Positioning wedge
- BriefLink is not pitched as generic link-sharing or a broad SMB productivity tool
- It is framed as a simple way to present the right client-facing materials in one clean briefing flow
- Core promise: **make agencies look sharper, clearer, and easier to work with without more ops overhead**

## 3) Messaging pillars

- **Clarity over clutter:** stop sending clients a pile of links with no narrative
- **Look more organized instantly:** improve perceived professionalism without redesigning your stack
- **Faster client momentum:** reduce stalled replies caused by confusing next steps
- **Consistent handoffs:** make proposals, kickoff resources, and updates feel standardized across accounts
- **Low-lift adoption:** use existing materials; package them better rather than rebuilding process from scratch
