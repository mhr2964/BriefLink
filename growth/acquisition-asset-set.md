# BriefLink 3-Channel Acquisition Asset Set

## Audience hypothesis
**Best-fit buyer right now:** small-to-midsize agencies and consultancies that repeatedly turn messy client conversations into scoped work, and feel friction in the gap between **call notes** and a **client-ready brief**.

### Who this is for
- Strategy, brand, web, product, and marketing agencies
- Fractional consultants and boutique consultancies
- Teams where founders, strategists, account leads, or producers still translate discovery calls into internal/external briefs manually

### Trigger conditions
They are likely to care if they:
- run frequent discovery, kickoff, or scoping calls
- lose time rewriting notes into usable briefs
- have inconsistent intake across team members
- need something shareable with clients or delivery teams immediately after the call
- feel CRMs are too heavy, note-takers too passive, and generic AI too unreliable for structured scoping

### Wedge
**BriefLink turns client conversations into a structured, shareable brief fast.**  
Not a CRM. Not just a transcript. Not a generic AI assistant. The immediate artifact is the value: a brief teams can review, refine, and share.

### Why now
- Agencies are already using AI to capture calls, but still doing manual work to make those calls actionable
- Buyers are fatigued by tools that collect data without producing a client-ready output
- Teams need faster turnaround between conversation and scope alignment, especially in leaner services businesses

### Why us
- We focus on the **brief** as the output, not notes, records, or dashboards
- We reduce the messy handoff from conversation to scoping artifact
- We are purpose-built for service businesses that sell and deliver through client conversations

## Cross-channel emphasis rules

### Emphasize
- The painful gap between conversation and usable brief
- Immediate practical output: a structured, shareable brief
- Service-business specificity: agencies and consultancies
- Relief from manual rewrite/scoping admin

### Exclude
- Generic productivity language for all SMBs
- Broad “capture every meeting” messaging
- Dashboard/analytics framing
- CRM replacement framing
- Overpromising autonomous strategy or proposal generation

Claims that should stay out of market-facing copy until confirmed:
- direct integrations

- automatic scope/proposal generation
- customer logos or named pilots
- broad “for every client meeting” positioning
