# BriefLink Growth Readiness Packet

## 1) ICP Summary and Wedge Hypothesis

### Primary ICP
Small-to-midsize agencies and consultancies that repeatedly turn messy client conversations into internal briefs, kickoff inputs, or delivery starting points.

### Best-Fit Buyers
- Agency founder or principal
- Client services lead
- Strategy lead
- Operations lead
- Consultancy partner overseeing discovery-to-delivery handoff

### Best-Fit Firm Traits
- 5–50 person service firms
- Multiple active client accounts at once
- Frequent discovery, intake, or kickoff work
- Client context spread across calls, notes, email, and chat
- Real cost when teams have to reconstruct what was said

### Core Pain
Important client context gets scattered. Teams then spend time pulling notes back together, aligning on what matters, and rebuilding a usable brief by hand.

### Wedge Hypothesis
BriefLink is most likely to win first with agencies and consultancies that already have enough inbound client information, but lack a clean way to consolidate it into a structured brief without adding another heavy system.

## 2) Core Launch Narrative Using Current MVP Only

### Positioning
BriefLink helps agencies and consultancies turn scattered client input into a clearer brief starting point.

### Narrative
Most service teams do not struggle to collect client information; they struggle to consolidate it. Discovery notes, emails, chat threads, and partial docs create friction before the real work starts. BriefLink focuses on that narrow, repeated problem: bringing scattered context together into a more structured brief the team can review, refine, and use for the next step.

### MVP-Safe Promise
- Bring scattered client inputs into one working view
- Reduce manual effort spent reconstructing discovery context
- Create a cleaner starting point for internal alignment or client-facing work
- Support a faster handoff from intake to brief drafting

### What We Can Say
- “Turn scattered client input into a clearer brief.”
- “Spend less time rebuilding discovery context.”
- “Create a more usable starting point for client work.”
- “Built for agencies and consultancies managing multiple client conversations.”

### What We Should Not Say
- “Fully automates client onboarding”
- “Replaces your project management stack”
- “Delivers perfect briefs without review”
- “Provides deep analytics on client performance”
- “Works for every small business workflow”

## 4) Initial Channel Plan

### Priority Channels
1. Founder-led outbound to agency and consultancy decision-makers
2. Warm intros through founder, operator, and agency networks
3. LinkedIn posts focused on briefing friction and handoff quality
4. Tight, low-volume email outreach to niche service firms

### Outreach Angle
Lead with the cost of reconstructing client context, not with generic AI or productivity language. The message should feel familiar to teams that run discovery often and lose time turning raw conversations into a usable brief.

### Best Initial Segments
- Brand and creative agencies
- Web and design studios
- Boutique strategy consultancies
- Messaging and positioning consultancies
- Research-led service firms with repeat client intake

### Suggested Offer
“Show us your current intake-to-brief workflow, and we’ll show how BriefLink can help turn scattered client input into a cleaner starting brief.”

### Early Success Signals
- Prospects immediately recognize the problem framing
- Teams can describe a repeated manual intake-to-brief process
- Buyers ask to compare BriefLink against their current note/doc workflow
- Conversations come from firms handling several active client threads at once

## 6) Mismatch Memo

### Buyers to Deprioritize
- Broad SMB audiences without a repeat client-brief workflow
- Large enterprises expecting procurement-heavy rollout
- Teams looking for full project management replacement
- Solo freelancers with low process repetition
- In-house marketing teams focused mainly on campaign execution

### Mismatch Signals
- Wants an all-in-one operating system
- Prioritizes reporting or analytics over briefing clarity
- Needs deep integration breadth on day one
- Has little repeated discovery or intake work
- Does not feel meaningful pain from scattered client context

### Messaging Traps to Avoid
- “For every business”
- “End-to-end workflow automation”
- “Replaces your current stack”
- “Instant perfect briefs”
- “AI strategist for your entire agency”

### Internal Guardrail
If the campaign does not clearly connect to repeated agency or consultancy intake and briefing pain, it is out of scope for launch.

**Shippability Verdict:** Shippable for targeted outreach and early launch materials if used with workflow-led demos and without quantified performance claims.
