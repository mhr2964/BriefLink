# BriefLink waitlist email sequence

## Audience
Agency and consultancy operators, founders, and delivery leads joining the early-access waitlist.

---

## Email 1: Waitlist confirmation

**Subject:** You’re on the BriefLink early list

Hi `{first_name}`,

You’re in.

BriefLink is built for agencies and consultancies that need a faster way to turn client conversations, requests, and decisions into a clear brief the team can act on.

We’ll send occasional updates, workflow examples, and early-access details as spots open.

If you want, just reply with your firm type and team size so we can send the most relevant examples.

— `{sender_name}`

---

## Email 2: Value reminder

**Send:** 3–5 days after signup

**Subject:** What BriefLink is meant to help with

Hi `{first_name}`,

A quick note while you’re on the waitlist.

BriefLink is most useful when client information is spread across calls, docs, email, and Slack — and someone on the team has to turn that into a clean handoff or brief.

Good early-fit use cases:
- post-call recap into a client-ready brief
- scattered feedback into one alignment doc
- strategy-to-delivery handoff cleanup
- revision-round summary

We’ll share more as access opens.

— `{sender_name}`

---

## Email 3: Access prep / soft activation

**Send:** 5–7 days after email 2 or when launch momentum is active

**Subject:** A simple way to evaluate BriefLink

Hi `{first_name}`,

When you get access, the best way to evaluate BriefLink is with one real client workflow — not a fake example.

Start with something recent:
- a client call
- a request thread
- a messy feedback round
- an internal handoff that needs cleanup

That usually makes the value obvious fastest.

We’ll be in touch as access opens.

— `{sender_name}`

## Notes
- Replace `{first_name}` and `{sender_name}` before sending.
- Optional segmentation fields: firm type, specialization, team size, role.
- Keep this sequence plain-text friendly for founder-led sending.
