# BriefLink Launch Implementation Pack

## Purpose
This document translates the locked BriefLink baseline into an implementation-ready handoff for growth-owned acquisition touchpoints. It is the source guide for launch copy application, QA review, and channel consistency. It is not a deployment artifact.

## Locked Positioning Baseline
BriefLink is positioned around calm client preparation and account risk visibility for agencies and consultancies with client-service leadership responsibility.

### Core Wedge
- Prepare with a clear brief.
- Spot account risk before client calls.
- Help client-service leaders go into important conversations with better visibility, less scrambling, and clearer next steps.

### Audience Guardrail
Target:
- agency leaders
- consultancy leaders
- account directors
- client partners
- strategy or delivery leads with client-service responsibility

Do not broaden toward general SMB productivity, generic team collaboration, or all-purpose work management. Do not center the message on approval cycles, review workflows, or scattered feedback chains.

### Outcome Guardrail
Emphasize:
- better preparation before client calls
- clearer visibility into account risk
- more confidence for client-service leadership
- calmer internal coordination around upcoming client moments

Avoid:
- generalized productivity improvement
- creative review optimization
- revision management
- project management replacement claims
- vague collaboration promises

## 8. Handoff Notes for Implementation Teams

### For Design / Web
- Keep the page visually centered on composure, readiness, and clarity.
- Ensure leadership context is obvious quickly from the hero and supporting copy.
- Avoid visuals or copy cues that imply task management or review tooling.

### For CRM / Lifecycle
- Send confirmation immediately after signup.
- Keep cadence light and purposeful.
- Segment primarily by role or firm type if needed, not by generic SMB categories.

### For Founder / Manual Outreach
- Personalize by role, agency type, or client-service context where possible.
- Keep the tone low-pressure and specific.
- Send traffic to the same early-access destination used across channels.

### For QA / Launch Ops
- Review all growth-owned surfaces against the locked CTA and terminology standards.
- Reject any copy that drifts into approvals, feedback loops, or broad productivity framing.
- Treat this document as the launch implementation baseline until a revised version is approved.
