# BriefLink Landing Page Messaging Brief
## Objective
Drive qualified agency and consultancy visitors to create a brief and reach the first meaningful activation milestone: **share a brief with a client or teammate**.

## Audience
Primary:
- Small to midsize agencies
- Consultancies
- Client-service teams that repeatedly gather project inputs from clients

Secondary:
- Boutique studios
- Fractional strategists and operators managing multiple client intake flows

Do not target:
- Broad SMB owners
- Internal-only corporate workflow teams
- Generic form-builder buyers
- Consumers or solo hobby projects

## Positioning
BriefLink helps agencies and consultancies turn scattered project intake into a single shareable brief they can send to clients and collaborators.

This is not a full project management platform, not a deep workflow automation suite, and not a claim about end-to-end delivery operations. The page should stay anchored on one immediate outcome: **getting a clear brief shared faster**.

## Core Conversion Path
1. Visitor understands BriefLink is for client-service teams
2. Visitor believes they can create a usable brief quickly
3. Visitor sees that the first payoff is sharing that brief
4. Visitor clicks primary CTA to start creating a brief

## Messaging Strategy
Lead with the operational pain of collecting requirements across email, docs, and chats. Resolve that pain with a narrow promise: create one clear brief and share it. Keep the language concrete, workflow-oriented, and believable.

Avoid visionary or category-creating language. Avoid implying AI automation, deep integrations, approvals, reporting, or full agency OS capabilities unless already confirmed in product.

## Benefit / Proof Blocks

### Block 1: Replace scattered intake
**Headline:** Stop piecing together project requirements from everywhere  
**Body:** Collect the details that usually live across email threads, call notes, chat messages, and docs into one working brief.  
**Proof style:** Show common source fragmentation, not quantified outcomes unless validated.

### Block 2: Create clarity earlier
**Headline:** Build a brief people can understand quickly  
**Body:** Structure key project information in one place so the next conversation starts with a clearer shared reference.  
**Proof style:** Emphasize readability, alignment, and handoff readiness.

### Block 3: Share the brief sooner
**Headline:** Get to a shareable brief without extra admin  
**Body:** Move from rough intake to something you can send to a client or teammate without rebuilding the brief in another tool first.  
**Proof style:** Anchor on reduced duplication and faster handoff.

### Optional Block 4: Built for client-service work
**Headline:** Made for agencies and consultancies  
**Body:** BriefLink is designed for teams that repeatedly gather client requirements and need a simple way to turn them into a usable brief.  
**Proof style:** Category fit, not company-size generality.

## Objections to Address

### Objection 1: “We already use docs/forms/email.”
Response framing:
- Acknowledge existing tools
- Position BriefLink as a simpler way to bring project inputs into a shareable brief
- Do not claim replacement of every workflow tool

Suggested copy:
**If your intake already starts in email, notes, or docs, BriefLink helps turn that messy early input into a clearer brief you can share.**

### Objection 2: “This looks too lightweight for our process.”
Response framing:
- Reframe around early-stage clarity, not full process depth
- Emphasize usefulness before complexity

Suggested copy:
**BriefLink is focused on the part that often breaks first: getting the brief clear enough to share and align on early.**

### Objection 3: “We’re not looking for another big system.”
Response framing:
- Position as narrow, immediate-value workflow
- Avoid implementation-heavy messaging

Suggested copy:
**Start with a single brief. The value shows up as soon as your team or client can review one clear version instead of chasing details across tools.**

### Objection 4: “Is this for agencies like us?”
Response framing:
- Use agency/consultancy language repeatedly
- Show repeatable client intake relevance

Suggested copy:
**Built for agencies and consultancies that handle repeated client intake and need a cleaner way to move into project definition.**

## Messaging Guardrails

### Claims We Can Safely Make
- Helps organize scattered client/project input
- Helps create a clear brief in one place
- Supports sharing the brief with clients or teammates
- Built for agencies and consultancies
- Reduces the need to rebuild the same brief across multiple early-stage artifacts

### Claims to Avoid Unless Product Confirms
- End-to-end project management
- Workflow automation
- AI-generated briefs
- Deep integrations
- Approval routing
- Revenue growth guarantees
- Time-saved percentages
- Team alignment metrics
- “Single source of truth” if product reality is still narrow
- Replacing PM tools, CRM, docs, or forms entirely

### Tone Guardrails
Do:
- Be operational and specific
- Name agency/consultancy use cases
- Focus on the first shareable brief
- Use believable, concrete language

Do not:
- Use broad “for every business” messaging
- Speak to undifferentiated SMB audiences
- Overpromise transformation
- Sound like enterprise workflow software
- Drift into generic productivity copy
